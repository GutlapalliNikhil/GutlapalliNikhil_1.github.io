Collaborative Robotics on Navigation Platform

According to the World Health Organisation(WHO), about 2 billion people are aged 60 years, 125 million above 80 years, and 65 million are physically handicapped in this World[16]. These people are in need of assistance to do their personal activities. So, we came up with an idea of developing an Autonomous wheelchair with an integrated voice and robotic arm on it. In this project, we aim at developing our own library for navigation, the study of wheelchair dynamics of wheelchair combined with a robotic arm, and validating the results and library using hardware. This overall system aims at performing Human-Robot-Interaction and Cooperation, Navigation and Mapping in dynamic environments, Opening a Door, Exchanging the objects with a Companion.

General Purpose Service Robot

Nowadays Home Service Robots are taking a great attention to the market as these robots can serve the elderly and disabled people. Human assistance can be a solution but labor scarcity and labor costs have been the major problems in most of the countries. In addition to this, human negligence and mistakes would risk the lives of the elderly and disabled. This is where these robots overcome humans and has emerged as the best solution to assist the elderly and disabled people in home and hospital environments. The project aims at developing an autonomous system with a highly sophisticated architecture built with robust features such as Human-Robot interaction, Navigation and obstacle avoidance, Object recognition under natural light conditions and Robot manipulation. The whole system is built using the ROS (ROS) framework, a meta operating system that runs on Ubuntu platform. ROS provides data transmission between the process, device control and hardware abstraction.
